ABOUT

P rof. Sabita Acharya started his academic career as Lecturer in Berhampur University in 1988 and joined Konark University, Bhubaneswar in 1990. He completed his long journey in Konark University for 32 years successfully delivering all his academic as well as administrative assignments including being positioned as Professor & Head of the Department of Business Administration, Dean of Faculty of Business Studies, Member of Syndicate etc.

He has guided more than 50 Ph.D Scholars; Authored more than 100 research papers and six books and course materials for IGNOU & Odisha State Open University; Attended more than 150 Seminars and chaired in many of them; Completed research projects funded by UGC, ICSSR, MCL, TATA STEEL, IOCL, OMFED etc.; Conducted several training programmes for Corporate Houses; Honoured with prestigious POSCO Asia Fellowship in 2007, Commonwealth Academic Fellowship in 2013 and awarded National Rural Talent Scholarship (1973-77) & National Scholarship (1977-81) for outstanding works.

  • A university is a large and diverse institution of higher education that plays a crucial role in advancing knowledge, fostering intellectual growth, and preparing individuals for professional and personal success. Here is a comprehensive description of a university:
  • 1. Academic Diversity: Universities are known for offering a wide range of academic programs and disciplines, including arts, sciences, engineering, humanities, social sciences, business, and more. They typically consist of multiple colleges or faculties, each specializing in specific areas of study.
  • 2. Research Hub: Universities are centers of research and innovation, conducting groundbreaking research across various fields. They house laboratories, libraries, and cutting-edge facilities where faculty, students, and researchers collaborate to address complex problems and push the boundaries of knowledge.
  • 3. Faculty Expertise: A university boasts a diverse and highly educated faculty, often composed of professors, researchers, and experts in their respective fields. They contribute to the academic community through teaching, research, and publications.
  • 4. Students: Universities attract students from diverse backgrounds, creating a rich and multicultural environment. Students pursue undergraduate, graduate, and sometimes doctoral degrees, engaging in academic programs that suit their career aspirations and personal interests.
  • 5. Campus Facilities: A typical university campus features various amenities, including classrooms, lecture halls, libraries, research centers, sports facilities, dormitories, dining halls, and recreational areas. These spaces foster a conducive learning and living environment.
  • 6. Lib, cultural, and economic development.
